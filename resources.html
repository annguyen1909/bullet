Impact Formulas + Examples

  • Action + What + How + Impact + Metric — “Optimized checkout flow via server‑side rendering, increasing conversion +19%.”
  • Problem → Action → Result — “High p99 latency → refactored hot paths and added caching → p99 −42%.”
  • Scope + Action + Scale + Outcome — “Led 8‑person team to ship onboarding in 4 weeks, growing WAU +18%.”
  • Tech + Scale + Perf — “Engineered Node.js + Redis service handling 3k RPS; p95 from 120ms → 60ms.”

Use concrete numbers (%, counts, ms, $) and timeframes (in 6 weeks, in Q3) to anchor impact.

Metrics Benchmarks by Role

Engineering

  • Latency: p95 or p99 reduced by 20–60% after optimization/caching.
  • Scale: services handling 1–5k RPS, HA 99.9–99.99%.
  • Reliability: error rate reduced by 30–70%, incidents per quarter down.

Product

  • Activation: signup→first action +10–30%.
  • Conversion: trial→paid +10–35% after experiments.
  • Retention: churn −2 to −6 pts over 1–2 quarters.

Growth/Marketing

  • Acquisition: +20–50% organic traffic via SEO content, technical fixes.
  • Virality: referral sign‑ups 1.5–3× with shareable templates.
  • Email: reactivation +10–25% via lifecycle nudges.

FAQ

Do these templates pass ATS?

Yes — they use semantic headings, standard fonts, and simple layouts that parse cleanly.

Can I use these verbs even as a junior?

Yes. Choose verbs that accurately reflect your contribution and scope; avoid inflating impact.

How many bullets per role is ideal?

3–6 per role, ordered by impact; each with a specific result or metric where possible.
